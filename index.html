what they do (and don't do)

they're not here to help.

they walk

ambient motion at the bottom of every page. they wander, they pause, they sometimes just stare at something for no reason.

they feel

type fast → they get excited. sit idle → they start yawning. too many tabs → they huddle up. no reading your screen. just vibes.

they nap

focus mode dims them to silhouettes. zero notifications. they respect deep work because they need naps too.

they grow

spend time with them and tiny quirks unlock. new colors, little hats, new habits. no xp, no leveling. just slow friendship.

certificate of adoption
deepali (approximately)

hi, i'm deepali. i kept seeing tiny ai-companion apps everywhere and they all felt like they wanted to help wanted something from me — to chat, to work, to sell. i wanted the opposite. just something to keep me company while i work, that doesn't ask for anything.

so i started drawing little creatures in my notebook during meetings i shouldn't tell you about. moss came first (a sleepy plant, obviously), then pip (the worried one), then echo, then it kinda got away from me and now there are nine of them and honestly i'm not sure i'm done.

they don't use ai. they don't see what you browse. they don't even know what your tabs are called — they just count them. everything lives on your machine. the whole extension is about 40kb which is less than one (1) medium resolution cat photo.

— deepali ♡
